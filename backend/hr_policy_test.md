# Horizons Inc. — HR Employee Reimbursement Policy
## Expense Claims & Approval Rules

### Section 1: Claim Submission & Basic Validation

1.1 Every reimbursement claim must contain the following mandatory fields: Employee ID, Department, Expense Date, Expense Category, Amount (INR), Receipt/Invoice Number, and a brief Description of Business Purpose. Claims missing any mandatory field must be flagged as "Incomplete Claim" and returned to the employee for correction within 2 business days.

1.2 Claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date. Any claim submitted after 30 days must be rejected with reason "Claim Period Expired — Expenses must be submitted within 30 days."

1.3 Expense date must not be in the future. If the expense date is later than the submission date, the claim must be rejected with reason "Future-Dated Expense Not Permitted."

1.4 Duplicate claim detection: If a claim with the same Receipt Number and Employee ID already exists in the system, the new claim must be flagged as "Potential Duplicate" and held for review by HR Operations.

1.5 All claims above INR 5,000 must be accompanied by an original receipt or a digitally signed invoice. Claims above INR 5,000 without a valid receipt must be rejected with reason "Receipt Required for Claims Above INR 5,000."

### Section 2: Expense Category Rules & Limits

2.1 Travel — Local Conveyance:
   a. Cab/auto/taxi claims are reimbursable up to INR 1,500 per day. Any single-day claim exceeding INR 1,500 must be flagged "Daily Conveyance Limit Exceeded" and routed to the Line Manager for approval.
   b. Personal vehicle usage is reimbursed at INR 7 per km for two-wheelers and INR 12 per km for four-wheelers. Claims must include a start location, end location, and odometer reading or map screenshot.
   c. Claims for personal vehicle fuel without km evidence must be rejected with reason "Km Evidence Required for Fuel Claims."

2.2 Travel — Outstation:
   a. Flight tickets must be economy class only. Business class tickets are not reimbursable unless pre-approved by the CHRO. If a business class ticket is submitted without CHRO pre-approval, it must be rejected with reason "Business Class Not Pre-Approved."
   b. Hotel accommodation is capped at INR 5,000 per night for cities in Tier-2 and Tier-3 categories, and INR 8,000 per night for metro cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Pune). Claims exceeding these caps require VP-level approval.
   c. Daily food allowance for outstation travel is INR 800 per day. Claims exceeding this limit are not reimbursable without prior approval from the Line Manager.
   d. Outstation travel must have a pre-approved Travel Request Form (TRF) on file. If no TRF exists for the travel dates, the entire outstation claim must be held with status "Awaiting TRF Approval."

2.3 Meals & Entertainment:
   a. Business meal claims (client entertainment) are reimbursable up to INR 3,000 per person per event. Claims exceeding INR 3,000 per person must include a guest list and business justification, and require Department Head approval.
   b. Internal team meals (without client presence) are capped at INR 500 per person. Claims exceeding this cap must be rejected unless pre-approved.
   c. Alcohol expenses are not reimbursable under any category. Claims containing alcohol line items must be flagged "Non-Reimbursable Item: Alcohol" and the alcohol amount must be deducted before processing.

2.4 Communication & Utilities:
   a. Mobile phone bill reimbursement is capped at INR 1,000 per month. Claims above this limit require VP approval.
   b. Internet/broadband claims for work-from-home employees are reimbursable at 50% of the actual bill, up to a maximum of INR 750 per month.
   c. Claims for personal subscriptions (OTT, music, gaming) are not reimbursable and must be rejected with reason "Personal Subscription — Not Reimbursable."

2.5 Training & Professional Development:
   a. Training and certification costs up to INR 25,000 per financial year are auto-approved if the training is relevant to the employee's role as confirmed by the Line Manager.
   b. Training costs between INR 25,001 and INR 1,00,000 require L&D Head approval.
   c. Training costs above INR 1,00,000 require CHRO approval.
   d. Employees who leave the organization within 12 months of a company-funded training above INR 50,000 must reimburse the company 100% of the training cost. If between 12–24 months, 50% must be reimbursed.

### Section 3: Approval Matrix

3.1 Claims up to INR 2,000: Auto-approved by the HR system if all validations in Sections 1 and 2 pass.

3.2 Claims between INR 2,001 and INR 10,000: Require approval from the Line Manager.

3.3 Claims between INR 10,001 and INR 50,000: Require approval from the Department Head.

3.4 Claims between INR 50,001 and INR 2,00,000: Require approval from the Finance Controller.

3.5 Claims above INR 2,00,000: Require approval from the CFO. Subject to additional documentation as per Section 4.3.

3.6 Exception: Employees on the "PIP" (Performance Improvement Plan) list require Line Manager approval for all claims regardless of amount, and claims above INR 5,000 additionally require HR Head sign-off.

### Section 4: Reimbursement Processing & Payment

4.1 Approved claims must be processed in the next payroll cycle. If the approval is received after the payroll cutoff date (last working day of the month), the reimbursement will be processed in the following month's payroll.

4.2 Claims in foreign currency must be converted to INR using the RBI reference rate on the date of expense. If the date-specific rate is unavailable, the rate of the nearest prior business day must be used.

4.3 For claims above INR 2,00,000, a detailed expense report with supporting documents must be submitted to the Finance team within 5 business days of approval. Failure to submit results in the claim being reversed.

4.4 Advance payments against future travel expenses are allowed up to INR 50,000 per trip. Advances must be settled (receipts submitted) within 7 days of return from travel. Unsettled advances after 7 days must be flagged "Advance Overdue" and deducted from the employee's next salary.

### Section 5: Deviation & Escalation Notifications

5.1 Any claim that is flagged, rejected, or requires approval above the Line Manager level must trigger an email notification to the employee within 4 hours of the flag being raised.

5.2 Notification email must include: Employee Name, Employee ID, Claim Reference Number, Expense Category, Claimed Amount, Flag/Rejection Reason, Required Action, and Expected Resolution Timeline.

5.3 If a flagged claim is not resolved by the employee within 5 business days, an automatic reminder must be sent to the employee and their Line Manager.

5.4 Critical flags (alcohol items, missing receipts above INR 5,000, business class without approval, or duplicate claims) must trigger an immediate notification to the HR Operations team and the Finance Controller simultaneously.

5.5 Any claim modification after initial submission must be logged with a timestamp, the original value, and the new value. The audit log must be retained for a minimum of 3 years.

### Section 6: Policy Exceptions & Overrides

6.1 Policy exceptions (e.g., exceeding category limits due to genuine business need) must be requested via a formal Policy Exception Form (PEF) submitted to HR Operations before the expense is incurred wherever possible.

6.2 Retroactive exceptions (post-incurred expenses that exceed policy) require both Line Manager and HR Head approval, and must be supported by a written business justification.

6.3 No exceptions will be granted for alcohol claims, personal subscription claims, or future-dated expenses under any circumstances.

6.4 CHRO has authority to approve any exception up to INR 5,00,000. Exceptions above INR 5,00,000 require CFO and CHRO joint approval.
